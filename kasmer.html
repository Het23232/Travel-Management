Kashmir

Basic Information

Capital: Srinagar (Summer), Jammu (Winter)

Official Languages: Kashmiri, Urdu, Hindi, Dogri, English

Currency: Indian Rupee (INR)

Population: Approx. 12.5 million

Geography

Kashmir is a region located in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent, surrounded by the Himalayas. It is known for its breathtaking landscapes, lush green valleys, and snow-capped mountains.

Major Cities

  • Srinagar: The summer capital, famous for its Dal Lake, houseboats, and Mughal gardens.
  • Jammu: The winter capital, known for its temples and cultural heritage.
  • Anantnag: A historical town known for its gardens and scenic beauty.
  • Baramulla: A significant town in North Kashmir with rich cultural heritage.
  • Gulmarg: A famous hill station and ski resort.

Famous Landmarks

  • Dal Lake: The iconic lake with houseboats and shikaras.
  • Gulmarg: A premier ski resort and winter tourist attraction.
  • Pahalgam: Known for its scenic beauty and base for the Amarnath Yatra.
  • Vaishno Devi Temple: A revered Hindu pilgrimage site in Jammu.
  • Shankaracharya Temple: An ancient Hindu temple overlooking Srinagar.
